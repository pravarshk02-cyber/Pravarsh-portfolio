About Me

My path into computer science started with a simple question: how do we get machines to learn, reason, and adapt? That question turned into a focus on Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, where I enjoy taking abstract ideas — matrices, gradients, probability distributions — and turning them into systems that actually work.

I care about building things efficiently, not just building them. Calculus and linear algebra aren't just subjects I study for exams; they're tools I reach for when reasoning about how a model learns, which makes optimizing or debugging a system feel less like guesswork and more like solving an equation.

I'm currently a Computer Science student at Vaagdevi Engineering College, strengthening my foundation across software engineering and the mathematical core of ML — one project at a time.
